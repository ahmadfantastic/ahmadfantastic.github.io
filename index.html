Research

I develop adoption-ready project-based learning (PjBL) resources and study how students and instructors use them in real computing courses. My doctoral work includes FORAP, an expert-validated framework for organizing reusable and adaptable PjBL projects, and a portfolio of 14 NSF-funded computing projects adopted at seven U.S. and Canadian universities. I also study how LLMs can support project scaffolding, assessment, and software engineering workflows without replacing students' individual reasoning.

  • Adoption-ready PjBL: project design, technical support, faculty adoption, and reusable project packages
  • Assessment of higher-order thinking through transfer-oriented design problems and learner profiling
  • Applied LLMs: prompt engineering, RAG, fine-tuning, agents, and human-in-the-loop educational tools
  • Empirical software engineering for privacy/log analysis, bug-report decomposition, and AI-assisted workflows
